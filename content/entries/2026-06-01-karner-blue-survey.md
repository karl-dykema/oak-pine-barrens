First adult flight survey of the season. Conditions: clear, 68°F at 8 AM, light south breeze. Peak flight time for first brood is typically 9–11 AM.

## Survey Method

Walked 5 established transects (50m each) through the primary lupine patches. Counted all Karner blues within 2.5m on either side. Recorded sex when possible.

## Results

| Transect | Length | KB Adults | Male | Female | Notes |
|----------|--------|-----------|------|--------|-------|
| T1       | 50m    | 4         | 3    | 1      | Active nectaring |
| T2       | 50m    | 2         | 2    | 0      |        |
| T3       | 50m    | 7         | 4    | 3      | Oviposition observed |
| T4       | 50m    | 1         | 1    | 0      |        |
| T5       | 50m    | 0         | 0    | 0      | Shaded, poor lupine |

**Total: 14 adults** (10 male, 4 female)

This is a promising count for a site that hasn't had active management. The T3 oviposition observation is particularly encouraging — females were placing eggs on lupine leaves in the sunnier central opening.

## Lupine Condition

Peak bloom. Roughly 60% of rosettes have active flower spikes. Density is highest in Patch A (T3 transect) — estimated 400+ rosettes in a 20m × 15m area. Patch B (T1-T2) is smaller but appears to have better connectivity to bare sand.

## Notes

Second brood survey scheduled for late July. Will coordinate with MDNR permit requirements before that survey.
