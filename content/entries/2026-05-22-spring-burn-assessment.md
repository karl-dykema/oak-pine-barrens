Met with Tyler from the state DNR fire crew to assess conditions in Unit 1 ahead of a potential prescribed burn window in late June. Current fuel load is heavy — 2–3 years of accumulated fine fuels with decent jackpine needle cast underneath the shrub layer.

## Fuel Assessment

Fine fuels are present and contiguous in the open areas. The buckthorn thickets reduce fire spread potential in the interior but should torch well if we get dry conditions. Fuel moisture at time of assessment was around 14%, which is on the drier end for mid-May.

## Burn Window Discussion

Tyler estimated a late-June window is viable if we hit two consecutive dry days before ignition. The target parameters we discussed:

- Relative humidity: 35–50%
- Wind: 5–12 mph, south to southwest
- Temperature: under 80°F
- Fuel moisture: 12–18%

Spring burning is preferred for lupine response — burns after seed set can reduce lupine density in the short term.

## Concerns

The two-track bisecting Unit 1 complicates control line setup. We'll need to wet the road margins before ignition. Also want to confirm the neighboring landowner to the north is notified — Rod is handling that conversation.
