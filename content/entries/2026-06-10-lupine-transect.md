Completed the formal lupine density transect survey to establish a pre-management baseline. This data will be repeated post-burn and in subsequent years to track habitat response.

## Method

Belt transects (2m wide × 50m long) placed at random azimuths through each identified lupine patch. Counted rosettes and flowering stems within belt. Also recorded microhabitat characteristics (% bare sand, % lichen cover, canopy cover estimate).

## Summary Results

**Patch A (East Opening):**
- 6 transects completed
- Mean rosette density: 8.3 per m²
- Flowering rate: 61%
- Bare sand: 22% cover, primarily along deer trails
- Canopy cover: 15%

**Patch B (West Sandy Area):**
- 4 transects completed
- Mean rosette density: 4.1 per m²
- Flowering rate: 55%
- Bare sand: 38% — best bare sand connectivity on site
- Canopy cover: 8%

**Patch C (Road Edge):**
- 2 transects completed
- Mean rosette density: 2.0 per m²
- Likely a sink population given road edge stress and mowing history

## Microhabitat Notes

Patch B has the most structurally appropriate habitat — good bare sand, low canopy, lichen-dominated ground layer (*Cladonia* sp.) interspersed with *Carex pensylvanica*. This patch should be the management priority.
