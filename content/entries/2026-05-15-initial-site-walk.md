Walked the full west parcel with Rod this morning. Conditions were dry — light dew, high around 62°F. Primary purpose was to establish a baseline before any management work begins.

## Overstory

Black oak (*Quercus velutina*) and jack pine (*Pinus banksiana*) dominate the open barrens areas, with a notable shift to red maple and black cherry in the swale that cuts through the northeast corner. Crown cover in the open areas is probably 20–30% — appropriate for barrens structure — but the shrub layer is heavily invaded.

## Shrub Encroachment

Glossy buckthorn (*Frangula alnus*) is the primary concern. Dense thickets in the interior, particularly along the two-track. Some black cherry (*Prunus serotina*) mixed in. No evidence of recent fire, which tracks with the land history — probably 20+ years since any burn activity.

## Ground Layer

Thin but present. Scattered lupine rosettes (*Lupinus perennis*) in the sandier openings along the east edge. Bird's-foot violet (*Viola pedata*) in a few spots. Bare sand pockets are encouraging — that's the target substrate for Karner blue habitat.

## Next Steps

- Flag transect lines for lupine survey
- Set up photopoints at 4 corners and the central opening
- Coordinate with Emily on burn unit mapping
